Introduction

  • Mutual coherence, $\mu(A)$, is a property of a matrix $A$.
  • Useful for compressive sensing.
  • Roughly: cosine of smallest angle between any two columns.
  • Precisely: if $A=[a_1,...]$
$$ \mu(A)=\max\limits_{1\leq k,j\leq m, k\neq j } \frac{|a_k^T a_j|}{\|a_k\|_2 \|a_j\|_2} $$

Intuition (1/2)

  • Consider the columns of $A$ as vectors
  • For example $$ A=\begin{bmatrix} \color{red}{1}&\color{blue}{-1}&\color{green}{1}\\ \color{red}{1}&\color{blue}{2}&\color{green}{4} \end{bmatrix} $$
  • Three vectors in two-dimensional space

Intuition (2/2)

  • Smallest angle between any of the vectors: $\theta \approx 31^\circ$.
  • Mutual coherence: $\mu=|\cos(\theta)| \approx 0.86$.
  • $\mu$ measures vector "spread".
  • $0 \leq \mu \leq 1$
  • Lower value means more spread.

Computation
  • Normalize columns of $A$:
    • sum of squares = 1;
    • call $A_n$.
  • $A_n^T A_n$:
    • has inner product of every pair of $A_n$'s columns;
    • largest inner product is $\mu(A)$;
    • (off-diagonal terms only).

Bound
  • 3 vectors in 2 dimensions:
    • Minimum angle $\leq 120^\circ$
    • $\mu\geq 0.5$
  • $N$ vectors in $M$ dimensions: $$ \mu\geq\mu_0 $$ $$ \mu_0 = \sqrt{\frac{N-M}{M(N-1)}} $$ $$ \frac{1}{M} \leq \mu_0 < \frac{1}{\sqrt M} $$
  • Easier to achieve small $\mu$ for larger $M$.
